10) Haikyuu

Haikyuu!! is widely considered one of the best sports anime of all time due to its phenomenal character development.
It gives meaningful arcs to not just the main duo but the entire cast, including rival teams. The show is amazing at
building hype and tension during its realistic volleyball matches while maintaining an incredibly positive and uplifting
tone about teamwork and self-improvement.

Trivia: The author, Haruichi Furudate, gave the two main characters, Hinata and Kageyama, names that create a contrasting
theme. What is this theme?

Their names relate to "light and shadow." Hinata (日向) contains the character for "sun" or "daylight," while Kageyama (影山) contains the character for "shadow."

9) Your Lie in April

This is a beautiful and heartbreaking anime known for its powerful emotional storytelling. It's a story of overcoming
trauma through music and human connection. The series is celebrated for its stunning, vibrant animation, especially
during the classical music performances, and its ability to weave a deeply moving narrative that is both tragic and hopeful.

Trivia: In the first episode, the animation includes a specific black cat that Kosei sees. This cat appears multiple
times throughout the series. What does it often symbolize?

The cat is often used to symbolize Kosei's late mother and his associated trauma with the piano.

8) Blue Lock

Blue Lock completely subverts the traditional sports anime. Instead of focusing on teamwork, its premise
is a "battle royale" to create the world's greatest "egoist" striker. It excels in its unique, hyper-individualistic
philosophy, its intense and dynamic art style, and the psychological pressure that makes it feel as much
like a thriller as a sports show.

Trivia: The unique, hexagonal-pentagonal design of the rooms and fields inside the Blue Lock facility is based on what real-world object?

The pattern of a traditional soccer ball.

7) Initial-D

A legendary series in the racing genre, Initial D is beloved for its technical accuracy regarding Japanese car
culture and drifting techniques. Its biggest strengths are the incredibly tense and exciting street races
and its iconic, high-energy Eurobeat soundtrack that has become synonymous with the anime itself.

Trivia: What is the real-world nickname for Takumi Fujiwara's iconic car, the Toyota Sprinter Trueno AE86, which is also used in the series?

The "Hachi-Roku," which is Japanese for "Eight-Six" (from the "86" in AE86).

6) The Fragrant Flower Blooms with Dignity

This story is praised for being an incredibly sweet and wholesome romance. It subverts typical
high school tropes by focusing on the genuine, respectful relationship between a boy perceived as a
delinquent and a girl from an elite school. Its charm lies in its lack of unnecessary drama and its focus
on mutual understanding and the characters' efforts to better themselves for each other.

Trivia: The two main high schools in the story, Chidori High and Kikyo High, are named after flowers. What flowers do they represent?

Chidori is named after the plover bird but is also a type of orchid, while Kikyo is the Japanese name for the Bellflower.

5) Bleach

Bleach is a cornerstone of battle shounen anime, celebrated for its iconic character designs and
unparalleled sense of style. Its strengths are its creative world-building centered around Soul Reapers
and the afterlife, and its epic, visually impressive sword fights. The "Soul Society" arc, in particular,
is hailed as one of the best arcs in the genre's history.

Trivia: In Tite Kubo's initial concept for the series, Rukia Kuchiki was supposed to be the main character, and she
was not meant to wield a sword. What was her weapon originally?

A scythe.

4) Code Geass

This anime is a masterpiece of strategy and mind games. It features a brilliant anti-hero protagonist
who wages war against a global superpower using his intellect and a supernatural ability. The show
is famous for its fast-paced, thrilling plot full of shocking twists, its exploration of complex moral
and political questions, and its strategic "mecha" battles that feel more like high-stakes chess matches.

Trivia: What real-world fast-food chain is famously and frequently featured throughout Code Geass due to a prominent product placement deal?

Pizza Hut. The characters are often seen eating their pizza, and the logo appears frequently.

3) Steins;Gate

Considered one of the best time-travel stories ever told, Steins;Gate is exceptional for its intelligent,
character-driven science fiction. It begins as a slow-burn slice-of-life that makes you deeply care about the cast
before transforming into a gripping psychological thriller. The show's handling of the consequences
and paradoxes of changing the past is both brilliant and emotionally devastating.

Trivia: The character John Titor, who communicates with the main characters about time travel, is not an original
creation for the anime. Where did this character originate?

John Titor was a name used on internet forums in 2000-2001 by a poster who claimed to be a real military time traveler from the year 2036.

2) Attack on Titan

AOT is renowned for its intricate world-building and profound mystery. It starts as a desperate,
post-apocalyptic struggle for survival and evolves into a complex story about war, freedom, and
the cyclical nature of hatred. Its strengths lie in its intense, high-stakes action, jaw-dropping plot twists,
and deep exploration of moral ambiguity, where the lines between hero and villain constantly blur.

The design of the towns within the walls, like the Shiganshina District, is heavily inspired by a real-life medieval
town in Germany that is famous for having a completely intact city wall. What is the name of this town?

Nördlingen, Germany.

1) Re:Zero

This series is a masterclass in psychological deconstruction of the "transported to another world" genre.
Instead of being an overpowered hero, the main character is exceptionally weak, with his only power being
the ability to "Return by Death" to a previous checkpoint. The show excels in its raw and realistic portrayal
of trauma, suffering, and a character's slow, painful growth in the face of despair.

Trivia: The name of Subaru's powerful but devastating ability, "Return by Death," was originally different in the
author's web novel drafts. What was it called?

It was initially called "Resets," which is a more direct video game term.
